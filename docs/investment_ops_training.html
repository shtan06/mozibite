PROPOSED TOPIC

 

Investment Operations - Innovation, Technology and Talent

 

Overview:

 

The objective of this course is to familiarize participants with investment operations and technology within the asset management landscape. Over the years, companies have been pouring substantial resources into ensuring regulatory compliance, sound risk framework and quality/accessibility of information. As such, we have seen significant growth in middle and back office functions, at which point asset managers may find themselves being weighed down by operational matters and unjustifiable costs. To address this issue, they need to have a transformation strategy that seeks to improve efficiency or off-load the burden to an outsourcing specialist.

 

The course aims to guide the participants on each strategy, identify key objectives and highlight potential issues. Implementing changes through shifts in technology and talent creates new processes and with it, new set of issues. By the end of the course, participants will be able to  understand how to manage the new processes  to keep costs in check and avoid being overwhelmed by superfluous technology, understand where to draw the line between manual process and automation   understand the pros and cons of outsourcing.

 

Objectives:

 

R Understand the roles and functions of middle and back office in an asset management company.

R Learn about investment management systems and third-party systems; what to expect from IT vendors, custodians and outsourcing partners.

R In what areas can technology address the challenges faced by asset managers, should they replace people, should they spend on AI & ML and how do you identify and resolve new problems when an organization shifts towards automation?

R Learn about outsourcing and how to map a strategy to decide between hiring talent, procuring systems and outsourcing.

R Understand investment data structures and data flow for effective data management and reporting.

R The future of reporting – reporting tools and programming languages. Database structures, ABOR vs IBOR, standard vs custom reports, paginated vs dashboard, data vs visuals, management/regulatory/BAU and R/Python/JavaScript/SQL.

 

 

Additional/Bonus Sessions (for Data Management and Reporting Group):

 

Investment reporting. Understand the core reports and learn to identify and manage data issues. Calculate bond yield, duration, day count and constant yield amortization using Excel, Power BI and SQL. Portfolio and security setup impact on cash flow, income accruals, average cost and valuation.

 

Sessions:

 

1.     The investment operation landscape – (a) from on-boarding to portfolio construction, trading, settlement and reporting. (b) the trade-cycle data flow, data management, reconciliation, pricing and corporate actions.

2.     Technology – investment systems, third-party systems, in-house systems, analytics systems, reporting tools and AI/ML.

3.     Procuring, writing RFPs, selection, contracts, implementation, vendor management and support.

4.     Business transformation – preparation for changes in workflow and new skills.

5.     Outsourcing vs in-house vs hybrid

6.     Issues and issues resolution
